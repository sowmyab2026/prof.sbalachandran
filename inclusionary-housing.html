Housing & Community Development

Inclusionary Housing in the United States

Research on the prevalence, practices, and production of inclusionary housing programs across U.S. jurisdictions.

Grounded Solutions NetworkRuoniu Wang

Overview

This work documents inclusionary housing programs across the United States and examines how program design, market context, and implementation capacity shape affordable housing production. It connects public-facing policy documentation with peer-reviewed scholarship on local housing policy outcomes.

Contribution

The project demonstrates that inclusionary housing outcomes depend on more than whether a policy exists. Local market conditions, affordability requirements, incentives, administrative capacity, and long-term stewardship all influence whether programs produce affordable units and how those units are distributed across communities.
