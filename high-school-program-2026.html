UPCD High School Program · Summer 2026

Seeing Your Community Through a Planner's Eyes

The 2026 program introduces students to planning foundations before they begin professional internship experiences with the City of Boston Streets Cabinet and related partners.

Program leadership

Program Director: Dr. Sowmya Balachandran.

Instructor: Elinor Hanjian, incoming PhD student in Environmental Science at UMass Boston with experience in environmental education, youth development, community-based research, teaching assistance, and alumni engagement.

Learning model

Students develop a “planner's eye” through daily observation, photography, mapping, discussion, and reflection. They examine how transportation, housing, public space, climate, infrastructure, and community engagement shape neighborhood life.

Classroom modules

Each module introduces a core planning issue and connects it to field observation or applied student work.

Planning foundations

What planners do and how planning shapes daily life.

Community assets

Identifying strengths, challenges, and opportunities.

Transportation

Complete streets, transit, biking, walking, and accessibility.

Housing

Housing types, affordability, development, and neighborhood change.

Climate resilience

Flooding, heat, trees, parks, and environmental justice.

Engagement

Listening, interviewing, surveys, and community voice.

Maps and data

Evidence, observation, visualization, and interpretation.

Planning proposal

Turning observations into public recommendations.
