Course

UPCD 730: Housing Policy and Community Development Finance

A course on housing systems, affordability, capital investment, community development finance, policy design, and the relationship between housing and neighborhood opportunity.

Course orientation

Students examine housing as both a market system and a public policy problem. The course connects housing need, land use, finance, federal and local policy, climate risk, community development institutions, and the social consequences of housing instability.

Selected learning outcomes

  • Analyze the historical evolution of U.S. housing policy.
  • Evaluate place-based and people-based housing strategies.
  • Understand affordable housing finance and development feasibility.
  • Assess climate risk, resilience, and housing vulnerability.
  • Connect housing policy to equity, community development, and regional planning.

Student research example

Students have examined the vulnerability of elderly and disabled residents in subsidized housing to sea-level rise and coastal flooding in Massachusetts.
