Research program

Housing, mobility, climate adaptation, and metropolitan governance.

My research examines the spatial and institutional systems that shape opportunity, vulnerability, and community capacity. Projects combine spatial analysis, policy analysis, institutional analysis, surveys, interviews, focus groups, administrative data, and community-engaged methods.

Research themes

Each theme links academic questions to applied planning problems faced by communities, public agencies, nonprofit organizations, and regional institutions.

Theme 1

Housing & Community Development

Affordable housing, homeownership, inclusionary housing, LIHTC, community development finance, and the institutions that stabilize households and neighborhoods.

Theme 2

Transportation & Mobility

Transportation access for older adults, people with disabilities, transportation-disadvantaged households, and nonprofit mobility systems.

Theme 3

Climate Adaptation & Healthy Communities

Coastal flood risk, managed retreat, healthy aging, subsidized housing, service access, and community continuity.

Theme 4

Regional Governance & Spatial Capacity

Spatial capacity loss, municipal fiscal stress, infrastructure burdens, regional interdependence, and governance mechanisms for adaptation.

Theme 1: Housing & Community Development

This work examines how housing policy and community institutions shape access to opportunity, wealth-building, neighborhood stability, and fair housing outcomes.

Community Infrastructure and Spatial Opportunity

This research critiques conventional opportunity mapping by showing how community-based support systems shape the feasibility of low-income and minority homeownership.

Open project story

Inclusionary Housing in the United States

With Ruoniu Wang and Grounded Solutions Network, this work documents inclusionary housing programs across U.S. jurisdictions and examines how program design, local market conditions, and implementation capacity shape affordable housing production.

Open project story

Fair Housing Litigation and Texas LIHTC Policy

This project studies how fair housing litigation shaped Texas Qualified Allocation Plans, deconcentration incentives, revitalization criteria, community opposition rules, and definitions of neighborhood opportunity.

Open project story

Theme 2: Transportation & Mobility

This work studies mobility as a condition of health, social participation, food access, public service access, and community life.

San Antonio One-Call/One-Click and Transportation Coordination

This applied research examined the institutional and infrastructure conditions needed to implement a One-Call/One-Click transportation system for older adults, people with disabilities, and transportation-disadvantaged riders in the San Antonio region.

Open project story

Metrocrest Unlocking Mobility Study

In collaboration with the University of North Texas and Metrocrest Services, this mixed-methods evaluation examined whether flexible transportation incentives could expand mobility for older adults and disabled riders beyond medical appointments.

Open project story

National One-Call/One-Click Systems

This research examines how mobility coordination systems are organized nationally, including host institutions, jurisdictional scale, target population, service model, funding arrangements, and actual transportation supply.

Open project story

Theme 3: Climate Adaptation & Healthy Communities

This work asks how climate risk intersects with aging, subsidized housing, mobility, health, and community support systems.

Healthy Aging, Coastal Risk, and Managed Retreat

In collaboration with UMass Boston Gerontology and the Urban Harbors Institute, this project examines how flood risk, housing vulnerability, mobility constraints, health needs, social networks, and service systems shape climate adaptation for older adults in subsidized housing.

Open project story

Subsidized Housing, Sea-Level Rise, and Special Needs Populations

Student research in Housing Policy and Community Development Finance examined the vulnerability of elderly and disabled residents in subsidized housing to sea-level rise and coastal flooding in Massachusetts.

Open project story

Theme 4: Regional Governance & Spatial Capacity

This emerging research agenda supports the intellectual foundation for my NSF CAREER proposal.

Spatial Capacity Loss and Regional Futures

This work develops a regional governance framework for understanding how hazards, infrastructure deterioration, insurance instability, demographic change, uneven investment, and fiscal dependence reduce the capacity of land and infrastructure to support housing, employment, public services, and community life.
