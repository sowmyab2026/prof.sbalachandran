Research

How do institutions, infrastructure, and spatial systems shape life chances?

My research examines the planning systems that produce or mitigate inequality, focusing on housing, transportation, environmental risk, community development, and regional governance.

Spatial justice

Opportunity mapping and planning practice

This work examines how planners define and measure spatial opportunity, with attention to construct validity, implicit racial bias, interpretability, and the difference between material access and symptoms of historical marginalization.

Housing policy

Affordable housing policy design

Research on inclusionary housing, LIHTC, fair housing litigation, tax credit allocation plans, and policy instruments that shape where affordable housing is produced and preserved.

Community development

Low-income homeownership and intermediary networks

Research on housing counseling, community-based organizations, faith-based institutions, social capital, wealth-building, and nonprofit support systems for low-income and minority homeowners.

Mobility justice

Transportation disadvantaged communities

Studies of One-Call/One-Click systems, community-based transportation, food access, medical transportation, and mobility barriers for older adults and people with disabilities.

Regional futures

Spatial capacity loss and regional governance

Emerging work on how hazards, infrastructure stress, insurance instability, fiscal pressure, and uneven development reduce the capacity of land and infrastructure to support housing, employment, and public services.

Pedagogy

Research-integrated education

Work that connects analytical methods, graduate studios, youth pathways, community partnerships, and practitioner training in planning and spatial analytics.
