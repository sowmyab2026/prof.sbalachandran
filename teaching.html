Teaching

Analytical rigor, public values, and professional planning practice.

My teaching prepares students to collect and interpret data, engage communities, evaluate policy, and produce professional planning deliverables for real clients and public audiences.

UPCD 620: Analytical Methods for Urban Planning and Community Development

A survey of qualitative and quantitative methods for planning, including statistics, regression, census data, interviews, focus groups, demographic projections, economic analysis, environmental impact assessment, and transportation analysis.

UPCD 730: Housing Policy and Community Development Finance

An examination of housing policy, capital investment, affordable housing development, market failures, place-based and people-based policy approaches, and community development finance.

UPCD 750/751: Urban Planning Studio

Project-based studios where students collaborate with governmental, nonprofit, and community partners to produce data-driven plans, reports, presentations, and implementation strategies.

Planning for Older Adults in Flood-Prone Subsidized Housing

An interdisciplinary studio focused on coastal flood risk, subsidized housing, older adults, managed retreat, vulnerability assessment, receiving-community readiness, and planning tools.

Youth pathways

Building pathways into planning.

My teaching and program development include research-integrated experiences for graduate students and pre-college pathways that introduce urban high school students to planning, spatial reasoning, community development, and public service.
