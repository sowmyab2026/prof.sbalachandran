Teaching

Preparing planners to analyze systems, work ethically, and serve communities.

My teaching emphasizes analytical breadth, professional writing, public communication, ethical judgment, community engagement, and applied problem-solving. Courses are designed for students preparing for planning practice, public service, research, advocacy, and community development work.

Flagship methods course

UPCD 620: Analytical Methods

A professional preparation course for urban practitioners. Students learn demographic analysis, Census and ACS data manipulation, economic analysis, environmental analysis, housing and land-use analysis, transportation analysis, public datasets, research article interpretation, and analytical memo/report writing.

Professional practice

Law, Ethics, and Planning Practice

A course on legal standards, ethics, professional responsibility, public participation, planning controversies, and the role of planners in democratic governance.

Open course page

Housing

UPCD 730: Housing Policy and Community Development Finance

Housing policy, capital investment, affordable housing development, market failures, place-based and people-based strategies, community development finance, and climate risk in housing systems.

Studio

Urban Planning Studio

Two-semester applied studios with public agencies, nonprofits, and community partners. Students produce reports, maps, presentations, and implementation strategies.
