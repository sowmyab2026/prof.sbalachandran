Course

Law, Ethics, and Practice in Planning

A professional planning course focused on the legal, institutional, and ethical foundations of public decision-making.

Course orientation

Planners work within legal and institutional systems that shape land use, housing, transportation, environmental decisions, civil rights, public participation, and community development. This course prepares students to understand those systems and to reason through professional dilemmas in ways that are legally grounded, ethically defensible, and attentive to equity.

Students examine planning law, constitutional foundations of land-use regulation, administrative processes, fair housing, civil rights, environmental law, eminent domain, ethics, professional responsibility, public participation, and contemporary planning controversies through real-world cases.

Learning goals

  • Understand the legal foundations of planning practice.
  • Evaluate ethical dilemmas using professional planning norms and public-interest reasoning.
  • Analyze how law, policy, and procedure shape planning outcomes.
  • Communicate professional judgments clearly to public, nonprofit, and community audiences.
  • Reflect on the role of planners in democratic governance, equity, and public accountability.

Law and institutions

Land-use authority, constitutional limits, administrative process, public hearings, regulatory tools, and implementation.

Ethics and responsibility

Professional conduct, conflicts of interest, transparency, participation, representation, equity, and accountability.

Practice cases

Real-world planning controversies involving housing, infrastructure, environment, redevelopment, displacement, and community voice.
