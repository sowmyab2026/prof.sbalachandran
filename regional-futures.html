Climate adaptation + regional governance

Spatial Capacity Loss and Regional Futures

Emerging research on how hazards, infrastructure stress, land constraints, fiscal dependence, insurance instability, and uneven development reshape municipal capacity and regional interdependence.

Overview

This research asks how metropolitan regions can maintain long-term economic resilience and socio-spatial continuity when some municipalities lose development capacity while others absorb redistributed housing, infrastructure, service, and fiscal pressures.

The work links parcel-level spatial analysis, municipal finance, regional network mapping, and simulation of municipal adaptation responses.

RO1

Typology

Classify municipalities by patterns of spatial capacity loss, fiscal vulnerability, infrastructure stress, and governance implications.

RO2

Interdependence

Map housing, labor, infrastructure, service, and community support networks that connect municipalities across metropolitan regions.

RO3

Adaptation

Model municipal adaptation choices and evaluate impacts on socio-spatial continuity across neighborhoods and municipalities.

RO4

Governance tools

Evaluate regional collaborations and develop planning support resources for practitioners.

Methods

  • Parcel and municipal spatial analysis
  • Regional network analysis
  • Scenario planning
  • Municipal adaptation simulation
  • Practitioner feedback and validation

Future outputs

  • Municipal typology maps
  • Regional dependency visualizations
  • Simulation demonstrations
  • Practitioner guides
  • Teaching modules and planning tools
