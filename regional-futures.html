Regional Governance & Spatial Capacity

Spatial Capacity Loss and Regional Futures

An emerging research program on how hazards, infrastructure stress, land constraints, fiscal dependence, insurance instability, and uneven development reshape municipal capacity and regional interdependence.

NSF CAREER trajectoryUMass BostonCollins CenterSustainable Solutions LabPaul English Applied AI Institute

Overview

This work develops a regional governance framework for understanding how hazards, infrastructure deterioration, insurance instability, demographic change, uneven investment, and fiscal dependence reduce the capacity of land and infrastructure to support housing, employment, public services, and community life.

The project asks how local capacity loss restructures regional interdependencies and redistributes development pressure, infrastructure burdens, and service demands across metropolitan systems.

RO1

Typology

Classify municipalities by spatial capacity loss, fiscal vulnerability, infrastructure stress, and governance implications.

RO2

Interdependence

Map housing, labor, infrastructure, service, and community support networks across metropolitan regions.

RO3

Adaptation

Model municipal adaptation choices and impacts on socio-spatial continuity.

RO4

Governance tools

Evaluate regional collaborations and develop planning support resources.
