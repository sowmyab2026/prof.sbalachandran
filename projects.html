Projects

Current research, planning tools, and public-facing scholarship.

These projects connect scholarly inquiry with planning practice, community engagement, and decision-support tools.

Flagship research agenda

Governing Regional Futures

Spatial Capacity Loss, Socio-Fiscal Dynamics, and Metropolitan Adaptation

This project develops a regional socio-fiscal governance framework to examine how environmental hazards, infrastructure stress, insurance instability, fiscal dependence, and uneven development reshape municipal capacity and regional interdependence.

Open project page

Climate & aging

Flood-Induced Managed Retreat among Older Adults in Subsidized Housing

This project examines flood risk, health vulnerability, housing instability, continuity of care, and receiving-community readiness for older adults in subsidized housing in coastal Massachusetts.

Transportation equity

Mobility for the Transportation Disadvantaged in San Antonio

A multi-report research effort on One-Call/One-Click systems, institutional readiness, infrastructure capacity, ridership needs, service gaps, and mobility barriers for older adults and people with disabilities.

Housing policy

Fair Housing Litigation and Tax Credit Policies in Texas

This project analyzes how fair housing litigation shaped Texas Qualified Allocation Plans, LIHTC siting incentives, affirmative marketing, revitalization criteria, and definitions of opportunity.

Youth pathways

Advancing Pathways for Youth of Color in Urban Planning

A public-facing educational initiative connecting high school students with urban planning, community development, public service, applied research, and local government practice.

Graduate studios

Applied Planning Studios

Studio courses with municipal, nonprofit, and community partners where students produce professional reports, spatial analysis, public presentations, and implementation-oriented planning deliverables.
