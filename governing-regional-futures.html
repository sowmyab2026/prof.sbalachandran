Flagship research agenda

Governing Regional Futures

Spatial Capacity Loss, Socio-Fiscal Dynamics, and Metropolitan Adaptation

How can metropolitan regions reorganize development capacity, fiscal resources, and governance arrangements to sustain long-term economic resilience and socio-spatial continuity under conditions of spatial capacity loss?

Overview

Environmental hazards, infrastructure stress, insurance instability, demographic change, and uneven development patterns are progressively reducing the capacity of land, infrastructure, and community systems to support housing, employment, and public services.

As these pressures intensify, some municipalities face declining development potential and rising fiscal burdens, while others confront unanticipated housing and service demand pressures. Although these challenges unfold across interconnected regional systems, adaptation decisions often remain fragmented among individual municipalities.

Central premise

Spatial capacity loss is not only a local vulnerability problem. It is a regional governance problem because housing markets, labor markets, infrastructure systems, public services, and community support networks cross municipal boundaries.

Research objectives

The project links spatial analytics, regional network mapping, municipal adaptation modeling, and planning support tools.

RO1

Typology

Develop a governance-oriented typology of spatial capacity loss across Massachusetts municipalities.

RO2

Interdependence

Map regional networks through which spatial capacity loss redistributes housing, labor, infrastructure, and service burdens.

RO3

Simulation

Model municipal adaptation decisions and socio-spatial continuity under alternative scenarios.

RO4

Governance tools

Evaluate purpose-driven regional collaborations and develop AI-assisted planning support resources.

Methods

  • Parcel and municipal spatial analysis
  • Regional network mapping
  • Scenario planning
  • Hybrid simulation of municipal responses
  • Practitioner feedback and validation
  • Planning support environment development

Outputs to add over time

  • Conceptual figures and typologies
  • Regional dependency maps
  • Simulation demonstrations
  • Municipal case profiles
  • Practitioner guides and micro-credentials
  • Reports, datasets, dashboards, and teaching modules
