Climate adaptation + aging

Older Adults, Subsidized Housing, and Managed Retreat

Community-engaged research on flood risk, housing vulnerability, mobility, health, social support, and receiving-community readiness among older adults in coastal Massachusetts.

Overview

This project examines how older adults in subsidized housing experience climate risk and what forms of planning, housing, transportation, and care infrastructure are needed to support safe adaptation and continuity of daily life.

The work combines spatial vulnerability analysis, focus groups, surveys, site-based fieldwork, and student research in coastal Massachusetts communities.

Research questions

  • How do coastal flood risk, aging housing, health conditions, and mobility constraints interact?
  • What forms of social infrastructure support continuity during climate adaptation?
  • How can receiving communities plan for care, housing, and service access?

Teaching integration

The 2025-2026 Urban Planning Studio engages graduate students in vulnerability profiles, focus groups, surveys, fieldwork, analysis, and planning recommendations for coastal communities.
