Transportation equity + aging

Metrocrest Unlocking Mobility Study

A mixed-methods evaluation of transportation incentives for older adults and disabled riders in partnership with Metrocrest Services in Carrollton, Texas.

Overview

This study explored the role of financial transportation incentives in mobility, health, social connection, housing, and financial outcomes among older adults and individuals with disabilities enrolled in the Metrocrest Services Transportation Program.

The evaluation compared a medical transportation control group with a Wallet App group that received transportation incentives allowing broader trip purposes.

Key insight

The most meaningful difference was not simply trip frequency but destination diversity. Wallet participants accessed a broader range of destinations, including grocery stores, charitable organizations, community services, and retail establishments.

Methods

  • Pre/post survey design
  • Program evaluation
  • Administrative trip record analysis
  • Health and quality-of-life measures
  • Spatial analysis of trip destinations and mobility footprints

Outputs

  • Final evaluation report
  • Pre-test survey report
  • Pre/post comparison report
  • Survey instruments
  • Spatial analysis of trip behavior
